Four GHI Indicator Values Over Time

India’s GHI Score Over Time

  • 2000: 38.1
  • 2008: 34.6
  • 2016: 29.3
  • 2025: 25.8

This shows that India has improved over time, but hunger is still classified as serious. So the problem is not solved, and strong intervention is still needed.

Four Indicators Used in GHI

1. Undernourishment

Percentage of people not getting enough calories for a healthy life.

2. Child Stunting

Low height for age in children under 5, showing chronic undernutrition.

3. Child Wasting

Low weight for height in children under 5, showing acute undernutrition.

4. Under-Five Mortality

Percentage of children dying before age 5, linked partly to poor nutrition and unhealthy environments.

Most Important Problem Areas

  • India has the second highest child wasting rate in the report.
  • Child stunting is also very high.
  • Child mortality has improved a lot since 2000.
  • Undernourishment improved overall, but hunger still remains a major national problem.

Why This Matters for the Food Rescue Project

Hunger is not only about shortage of food. It is also about nutrition quality, safe food access, last-mile delivery, and support for vulnerable groups.

  • Your project can reduce food waste by connecting surplus food providers with people in need.
  • It can improve food access at community level.
  • It can help NGOs, volunteers, and donors coordinate faster.
  • It can support better targeting of nutritious food for children, mothers, and poor households.

Useful Project Feature Ideas

Core Features

  • Location-based food donation and claiming
  • Priority distribution for vulnerable groups
  • NGO and community worker coordination
  • Real-time tracking of available food portions

Advanced Features

  • Nutrition-based food categorization
  • Safe food handling awareness
  • Last-mile delivery support
  • Special sections for child and maternal nutrition support

Suggested Interventions from the Data

  • Better access to social safety nets and food support programs
  • Focus on mother and child health, especially the first 1000 days
  • Better complementary feeding for children aged 6–24 months
  • Improved water, sanitation, and hygiene practices
  • Community-level participation and outreach
  • Promotion of nutritious and diversified food systems, including millets

Project Statement You Can Use

India still faces a serious hunger situation, with a GHI score of 25.8 and rank 102 out of 123 countries. Major concerns include 12.0% undernourishment, 32.9% child stunting, and 18.7% child wasting. A food rescue platform can help reduce food waste, improve food access, and support vulnerable communities through timely redistribution of surplus food.

Important Note

Do not compare rankings or scores across different Global Hunger Index report editions directly, because methodology, data sources, and country coverage may change. Valid comparison should use historical values given within the same report edition.
